# Cookie Policy

Intel Trader Technologies Limited uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit the Platform so that we can remember preferences, analyze usage, secure accounts, and deliver personalized and compliant services. This Policy explains how and why we use them, and how to manage your preferences.

### 1. What Are Cookies?
Cookies are small text files stored on your device’s browser. They can be “persistent” (stay for multiple visits) or “session-based” (deleted when you close the browser). Cookies may be set by us or by third parties whose services we embed.

### 2. Types of Cookies We Use

- **Essential Cookies:** Required for basic Platform functions such as authentication (NextAuth session cookies), subscription enforcement (usage limit tokens), and form submissions. Disabling these cookies likely prevents you from logging in or accessing paid features.  
- **Analytics Cookies:** We use analytics providers to understand how Nigerian traders use the Platform (page flows, feature engagement). Data is aggregated and used to improve UI/UX. We do not sell analytics data; sharing occurs only with processors under NDA.  
- **Functional Cookies:** These cookies remember settings (language, region, landing layout, cookie consent).  
- **Marketing/Tracking Cookies:** When we share links via email or integrate with marketing tools, we may use cookies to track campaign performance or to provide personalized content.  

### 3. Third-Party Cookies
We use trusted partners (e.g., Vercel deployment cookies, Google Analytics, Stripe for payment flows). Each third-party may have its own privacy practices; see their policies for details. We ensure that all third parties comply with relevant NDPR safeguards.

### 4. Managing Cookies
You can control or delete cookies via your browser settings. Each browser has instructions (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari). Blocking certain cookies may prevent you from logging in or using advanced features. Intel Trader does not rely on cookies for essential data transmission but does require them for authentication and UX continuity.

### 5. Cookie Consent and Changes
When you first visit the Platform from Nigeria or any other jurisdiction, we present a cookie banner to ensure compliance with local expectations. You may update your consent at any time by revisiting the banner, clearing cookies, or adjusting controls in your account settings. Changes to this Policy will be posted on this page with a revised “last updated” date; continued use implies acceptance.
